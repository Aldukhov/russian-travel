Traveling in Russia

The real country is not in the news, it's here.

Your shelf is the top one

What Haven't We Seen There?

According to VTsIOM surveys, 95% of Russians dream of going somewhere, but only 36% plan to spend their vacation in their home country. They say, why travel at home? In fact, Russia is a whole universe with the gentle sea of the south, dense forests of Sayan, and the harsh ice of the Putorana Plateau. And you can see all these beauties without millions in your account, a passport, and hours of flights. Like, for example, Vera Bashmakova, a brave young mother who took three children in tow, put them in her "Lada" and drove 20,000 kilometers across her native country. We have selected and described some interesting places worth your vacation.

Curonian Spit

URL

park-kosa.ru

Here, amid the forests and sandy dunes, you can see two water horizons - the calm Curonian Bay on one side and the ruffled waves of the Baltic Sea on the other. A unique natural zone on the edge of the Russian exclave.

Kaliningrad Region doesn't end here. For a traveler and explorer, right nearby is the westernmost point of Russia, the Baltic Spit, and the German heritage of small seaside towns. The atmosphere of these places excludes haste, immersing in the tranquility of nature and the scent of the steel, cool sea.

Kola Peninsula

URL

National Geographic

Almost the entire peninsula is located beyond the Arctic Circle. The Saami tundra, from which to the south is taiga, and to the north is the Arctic Ocean pretending to be the Barents Sea.

Perhaps you've watched Zvyagintsev's films and even heard the story of the Arctic festival in Teriberka. Maybe the word "Khibiny" hasn't been buried under the snow of school memories of geography lessons. Maybe you've never been interested in piercing the earth's crust with super-deep wells, and apatites have long covered you with apathy. But your dream of seeing the northern lights begins to come true with a ticket to Murmansk.

Altai

URL

YouTube

Altai is one of the most beautiful places in Russia. Primarily because of the mountains: if you travel along the ridge, you will see slopes covered with pines, mountain rivers, and lakes. And if you open the car windows, you can get acquainted with the invisible wonder of these places - mountain air.

The climate in Altai is temperate, so it's best to travel here in the summer. That way, you'll see all the diversity of the local flora and fauna. Moose roam through the forests of Altai, eagles fly over the ridges, and roe deer graze on the plains. And the famous Pallas's cats are also residents of the Altai Territory.

Winter Baikal

URL

https://vk.com/baikalmile

Everyone knows Baikal as the largest lake in the world. Many also know that it is the largest source of fresh water and one of the most beautiful places in Russia.

Of course, that's all true. But Baikal is also an ideal place for skijoring competitions. This is a sport where a skier attaches themselves to a motorcycle, and the tandem tries to achieve the highest possible speed on the ice. In March 2019, at the "Baikal Mile" festival, a world record was set - 197.011 km/h.

Karelia

URL

http://vodlozero.ru/

Siberia ends not at the Urals, but in Karelia: the Siberian larch that forms the taiga doesn't grow west of Lake Vodlozero. Instead, it grows here up to 30 meters - the forests of Karelian national parks have never known the axe because of the impassable swamps. Some pines here are already over half a millennium old. Touch the living creature that saw the sun earlier than Ivan the Terrible. In the virgin forest, you won't find a path for a hundred kilometers. And on rare footpaths, trees a couple of meters from the ground are marked with bear claws. So that everyone knows who the boss is here.
